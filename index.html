What the program hid

    Reflection

    This artifact extends Week 3’s question about what changes when human judgment becomes formal program. Instead of a harmless sequence problem, it stages a lethal decision. The human side makes uncertainty, disagreement, moral hesitation, and pressure visible. The formal side converts that same uncertainty into scores, confidence levels, and a recommendation.

    What surprised me in building it was not that the formal side looked more precise, but that its precision depended on hidden choices about what counted as evidence, how much uncertainty the system was allowed to tolerate, and which forms of risk were treated as acceptable. The program did not remove judgment. It reorganized it and made it appear cleaner than it was.

    This keeps the project tied to Week 3 by asking what changes when messy human deliberation is translated into formal procedure.
